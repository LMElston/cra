We may ask you for additional information

Once the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) has started reviewing your request, we may contact you if we need any help understanding what you provided, or to get any missing information.

How we review your request

It is a general CRA practice for another CRA official, or committee of officials, to review a request and prepare a decision report for the designated official's consideration, including a recommendation on whether or not granting relief is justified. It is the responsibility of the designated official to make the final decision and notify the taxpayer in writing.

Factors used in arriving at the decision

Where circumstances beyond your control, actions of the CRA, or inability to pay or financial hardship has prevented you from complying with your tax obligations, the following factors will be considered when determining whether or not the CRA will cancel or waive penalties or interest:

  • your history of compliance with your tax obligations
  • whether or not you knowingly allowed an arrears balance to exist upon which arrears interest has accrued
  • whether or not you exercised a reasonable amount of care in conducting your tax affairs, and whether or not negligence or carelessness has been demonstrated
  • whether or not you acted quickly to remedy any delay or omission

When you can expect a decision

Under normal circumstances, the CRA tries to issue a taxpayer relief decision within 180 calendar days of receiving the request. However, due to the COVID-19 pandemic, and a higher than normal number of requests for relief, it could take longer to process your request. For the most up to date information about our processing timeframe, go to Check CRA processing times.

If your request for relief is approved

If the CRA approves your request for relief, you will receive one of the following:

  • reduction in refunds
  • refund interest
  • application of refund to other debts
  • refund withheld until past due returns are filed

Find out more about the rules around credits and refunds in paragraphs 90 to 99 of Information Circular IC07-1R1

Recourse if your request is denied or partially approved

If you believe our decision was not fair and reasonable, there are recourse options available to you.

Second administrative review

You can ask for a second review online, see Submit your request for more information. Alternatively, you can send a letter to one of the following:

  • address provided in the decision letter you received
  • Intake Centre responsible for your province or territory of residence

A second review request requires the following:

  • the reasons why you disagree with the CRA's decision, for example, not all information was considered, certain facts or details were missing or misinterpreted or not considered in their proper context
  • any relevant new documents, new facts, or correspondence

Judicial review

If you still feel that we were not fair and reasonable, you can apply to the Federal Court for a judicial review of our decision. You must do this within 30 calendar days of the date you received the decision from the second review. To maintain fairness and transparency, the CRA official who completed the first review will not conduct the second review.

Requestion for remission review

If we deny your request to cancel or waive interest or penalties, you may be able to make a remission request.
