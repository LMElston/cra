How to claim it on your tax return

If approved, you can claim the DTC amount on your tax return:

  • To claim for yourself, see 31600 - Disability amount for self
  • To claim for your dependant, see line 31800 – Disability amount transferred from a dependant
  • To claim for your spouse or common-law partner, see line 32600 – Amounts transferred from your spouse or common-law partner

Medical practitioner charges

Any fees that the medical practitioner charges to fill out the form or to give us more information can be claimed as medical expenses on line 330 or line 331 of a tax return.

Processing time

Check processing times for you tax return using the CRA processing times tool.

Claiming for past years

If a person was eligible for the DTC for previous years but did not claim the impacted health amount when they sent their tax return, they can request adjustments for up to 10 years. There are a few ways to do this:

  • On your application, check question 3 of Part A to have the CRA adjust your returns
  • Send in a request to have your returns adjustment by the CRA for up to 10 years
  • Adjust your returns yourself, go to How to change my return
