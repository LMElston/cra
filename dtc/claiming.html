Who can claim the DTC

The Disability tax credit (DTC) is a non-refundable tax credit. You may claim it on your income tax return once the person with the impairment is approved. This may reduce the amount of income tax you have to pay. If the credit has no effect on your balance owing, the credit amount is not refunded to you.

You may claim the credit for:

  • yourself
  • a dependent
  • a spouse or common-law partner

We recommend that the person who completes Part A of the form be the one who claims the credit. Although this is not required, it may reduce the time we need to process your tax return.

How to claim it on your tax return

If approved for the DTC, you can claim the current year DTC amount on your tax return.

To claim for:

  • yourself use
    line 31600 - Disability amount for self
  • your dependant, such as a parent or child, use line 31800 - Disability amount transferred from a dependant
  • your spouse or common-law partner use line 32600 - Amounts transferred from your spouse or common-law partner

Medical practitioner fees

Some medical practitioners may charge a fee for completing the DTC application. However, you may be able to claim them as medical expenses on your tax return.

How long it will take

We aim to process your tax return within 10 to 12 weeks, once received by the CRA. It may take longer if information is missing or if you filed your application for the DTC at the same time.

CRA processing times

If you send your application for the DTC with your tax return, the CRA will review your application before assessing your tax return.

Claiming for past years

If you were eligible for the DTC amount for previous years but did not claim them, you can:

  • send in a request to ask the CRA to adjust your returns for you
  • adjust your returns yourself, go to change my tax returns

You have to claim your current year on your tax return.
