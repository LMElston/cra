Claiming the credit

On this page

Who can claim the DTC

The individual with the impairment may claim the DTC on their income tax return once the CRA has approved the application. This may reduce the amount of income tax they have to pay.

The DTC is a non-refundable tax credit. If the credit has no effect on the balance owing for the current year, the credit amount is not refunded.

Transferring the credit

If the individual with the impairment does not claim the entire credit on their tax return, they may transfer some or all of the credit to:

  • a parent or guardian
  • a spouse or common law partner

We recommend that the individual who claims the credit be either the individual with the impairment or the person whose SIN is on the application form (Part A number 2). This is not required, but it will reduce the time the CRA needs to process the tax return.

Changing who claims the transferred credit

The CRA requires written confirmation to transfer the credit to a parent or guardian, or spouse or common law partner whose SIN is not not on the application (Part A number 2).

Claiming the current year

Review the maximum 2020 disability amounts for people 18 and older, plus the additional youth supplement for those 17 and younger.

How to calculate your maximum 2020 disability amount:

18 and older: Option 1 of 2

  • $8,576 (base amount)
  • equals 8,576

17 and younger : Option 2 of 2

  • $8,576 (base amount)
  • plus $5,003 (additional youth supplement)
  • equals 13,579

How to claim it on your tax return

To claim the DTC for the current year, you must enter the credit amount on your tax return.

The person with the impairment may claim the credit for themselves, or transfer it to a parent or guardian, or to a spouse or common law partner.

Claim for yourself

If you are the person who qualifies, you may claim the credit for yourself.

Enter your credit amount in your tax return on:
Line 31600 - Disability amount for self

Claim for a dependant

If you are a parent or guardian of the person who qualifies, or you are claiming for a dependant.

Enter the credit amount in your tax return on:
Line 31800 - Disability amount transferred from a dependant

Claim for a spouse

If you are the spouse or common law partner of the person who qualifies.

Enter the credit amount in your tax return on:
Line 32600 - Amounts transferred from your spouse or common-law partner

Medical practitioner fees

If the medical practitioner charged a fee for completing the DTC application or for providing information, you may be able to claim it as medical expense on lines 33099 and 33199 of your tax return.

How long it will take

The CRA aims to process your tax return within 10 to 12 weeks from the time we receive it. It may take longer if information is missing or if you filed your DTC application at the same time.

Check CRA processing times

If you submit your DTC application with your tax return, we will review your application before assessing your tax return.

Claiming the past years

If you were eligible for the DTC in past years but did not claim it, you can adjust your previous tax returns for up to 10 years.

To claim the disability amount for past years, you may:

  • ask the CRA to adjust your previous return(s)
  • adjust your previous return(s) yourself using change my return

No action is needed if you checked the box on the application form asking the CRA to apply the credit to previous tax returns.

The CRA may provide the credit for past years as a payment.
