How to claim the credit on a tax return

You can claim the impacted health amount on your tax return once the person with impacted health is eligible for the DTC.

  • To claim the impacted health amount for yourself, see line 316.
  • To claim the impacted health amount for your dependant, see line 318.
  • To claim the impacted health amount for your spouse or common-law partner, see line 326.

Prior tax year claims

If a person was eligible for the DTC for previous years but did not claim the impacted health amount when they sent their tax return, they can request adjustments for up to 10 years under the CRA's Taxpayer Relief Provision.

To claim the impacted health amount for those prior years, you can ask for a reassessment. For more information, go to How to change my return.

Claiming fees chared by a medical professional to certify the DTC

Any fees that the medical practitioner charges to fill out the form or to give us more information can be claimed a medical expenses on line 330 or line 331 of a tax return.

Proposed rules

We published draft regulations that would limit the amount of fees that can charge for preparing DTC claims to $100. Refer to: Questions and answers to the proposed Disability Tax Credit Promoters Restrictions Regulations
