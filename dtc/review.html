Our review of your application

After we receive your application, we assess it to determine if you are eligible for the Disability tax credit (DTC). We base our analysis on the information given by the medical practitioner.

If we need more information, we may contact you or the medical practitioner.

How long it will take

We aim to process your application and send you a notice of determination within 8 weeks of getting your application. It may take longer if information is missing.

Check CRA processing times

If you send your application with your tax return, the CRA will review your application before assessing your tax return.

Replying to a clarification letter

We may send you a clarification letter asking for more information, such as supporting documents or receipts. The letter will contain a reference number and instruction on what you need to do.

You may send us the requested information using My Account.

Our decision about your application

We will send you a notice of determination to inform you of our decision.

If your application is approved

If your DTC application is approved, you may claim the tax credit on your income tax return. This may reduce the amount of income tax you have to pay.

Your notice of determination will show which year(s) you can claim the DTC. You don't need to re-apply for the credit every year, unless we tell you that you need to.

The DTC also opens doors to other federal, provincial, and territorial programs. Your notice of determination may include information about other programs that are dependent on DTC eligibility, such as:

  • Registered disability savings plan
  • Canada workers benefit
  • Child disability benefit

Most approved applications expire after a certain number of years and you will have to re-apply. When your eligibility is about to expire, we will notify you 1 year in advance, as well as in the year it expires.

You can view your DTC information in My Account.

You must tell us if your medical condition improves to the point that you no longer meet the criteria for the DTC.

If your application is denied

If your DTC application is denied, your notice of determination will explain why.

Check your copy of your application, Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate, against the reason(s) given. We base our decision on the information given by the medical practitioner.

Disagreeing with our decision

If you disagree with our decision, you still have options:

Call to discuss: Option 1 of 4

You may call us to ask questions or to discuss your application.

Contact the CRA about the DTC

Submit new documentation

You may send in relevant medical information that you have not already sent, such as new medical reports or a letter from a medical practitioner who is familiar with your situation.

The information you provide should describe how the impairment(s) affect you.

Submit new information using My Account.

Request a review

You can ask the CRA to review your application by contacting us in writing.

Your request must include relevant medical information that you have not already sent, such as new or updated medical reports, or a letter from a medical practitioner who is familiar with your situation.

Send your request by mail.

File an objection

You may formally object the decision by filing an objection within 90 days of getting your notice of determination from the CRA.

For more information, reveiw Brochure P148, Resolving your dispute: Objection and appeal rights under the Income Tax Act.
