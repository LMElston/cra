Who is eligible

Understanding eligibility

DTC eligibility is based on the effects of a mental or physical impairment, as certified by a medical practitioner. It is not based on a diagnosis or the presence of a condition.

You may be eligible for the DTC if you meet the criteria for 1 or more of the eligibility categories. These categories are based on activities and functions used in everyday living.

Select an eligibility category

Eligibility categories
Impairment category Description
Walking

You are unable to walk, or take 3 times longer than someone without an impairment, even with the use of therapy, medication, and devices.

Walking eligibility criteria
Mental functions

You are restricted in the mental functions necessary for everyday life, even with therapy, medication, and devices.

Examples: You have trouble remembering basic personal information or following simple instructions

Mental functions eligibility criteria
Dressing

You are unable to dress yourself, or take 3 times longer than someone without an impairment, even with therapy, medication, and devices.

Dressing eligibility criteria
Life-sustaining therapy

You require regular therapy to support a vital function.

Examples: Dialysis, insulin therapy, oxygen therapy, or chest physiotherapy

Life-sustaining therapy eligibility criteria
Feeding

You are unable to feed yourself, or take 3 times longer than someone without an impairment, even with therapy, medication, and devices.

Examples: You need help preparing food or have trouble chewing and swallowing

Feeding eligibility criteria
Eliminating (bowel and bladder functions)

You are unable to personally manage bowel or bladder functions, or take 3 times longer, even with therapy, medication, and devices.

Eliminating eligibility criteria
Hearing

You have trouble understanding spoken conversation with a familiar person in a quiet setting.

Hearing eligibility criteria
Speaking

When you speak, you have trouble being understood by a familiar person in a quiet setting.

Speaking eligibility criteria
Vision

You are considered blind in both eyes, even with the use of corrective lenses or medication.

Vision eligibility criteria
Cumulative effect (2 or more categories)

You have significant limitations in 2 or more categories (except life-sustaining therapy) that you combine to equal 1 marked restriction.
