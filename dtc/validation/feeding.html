Feeding eligibility

On this page

  • Overview

Overview

For some people, eating or feeding themselves can be a challenge even with the help of appropriate therapy, medication, and devices. The DTC aims to offset some of the costs caused by care, treatment, and related activities by reducing the amount of income tax you may have to pay.

Eligibility for the DTC is based on the effects of an impairment, not a diagnosis or the presence of a condition.

Example of people who may be eligible

Olga lost a limb and needs help to eat

Olga lives by herself.

Last year, she had an upper limb amputation. Olga now has a caregiver to feed her and to help her with her personal tasks.

Olga applied for the DTC. As a result of the information provided by the medical practitioner on the application form, she is now eligible for the tax credit because she is not able to feed herself.

Which medical practitioners can certify this
  • Medical doctor
  • Nurse practitioner
  • Occupational therapist

If you do not meet the criteria

If your impairment does not meet the eligibility criteria for a marked restriction, you may be eligible under the cumulative effect of significant limitations, which combines 2 less severe impairments.
