How to change your tax return

You may request a change to your tax return by updating the amount entered on specific line(s) of your return. You must have received your notice of assessment before doing this.

You may only request a change to a return for a tax year ending in any of the 10 previous calendar years. For example, if you make a request in 2022, it must relate to the 2012 tax year, or a later.

If you request a change, do not file another return for that year, unless the return you want to amend was a 152(7) factual assessment.

Adjust your return online or by mail

Online

My Account

Use the Change my return option in My Account to make online adjustment(s) for any of the 10 previous calendar years.

Exceptions to using My Account

You cannot use My Account to change:

  • a tax return that has not been assessed
  • a tax return where 9 reassessments exist for a particular tax year
  • a bankruptcy return
  • a return prior to the year of bankruptcy
  • carryback amounts such as capital or non-capital losses
  • a return of an international or non-resident client (including deemed residents of Canada, newcomers to Canada, and individuals who left Canada during the year)
  • the elected split-pension amount
  • a return where you have income from a business with a permanent establishment outside your province or territory of residence (you have to complete Form T2203, Provincial and Territorial Taxes for Multiple Jurisdictions)
  • a return that was filed by the CRA under subsection 152(7)

In any of these cases, go to by mail.

ReFILE

To use ReFILE, your tax return must have been filed electronically.

  • Individuals (NETFILE users) can use ReFILE to send adjustments for 2021, 2020, 2019 and 2018 returns. 
  • EFILE service providers can send adjustments for 2021, 2020, 2019 and 2018 returns. If you have questions about the functionality available in your software package, please contact your software provider.

Keep all your receipts and supporting documents in case the CRA asks to see them. Provide supporting documents only if we ask for them. Submit any documents we request using the method indicated in the request letter.

By mail

Complete and send both of the following to your tax centre:

  • a completed Form T1-ADJ, T1 Adjustment Request
  • all supporting documents for the change, including those for the original assessment, unless you have already sent them to the CRA

Send your current year return separately from any request to change a return for another year.

Processing time

Routine adjustment requests

We aim to send you a notice of reassessment, or a letter explaining why the CRA did not make the changes you asked for, as well as any refund within:

  • 2 weeks (online requests)
  • 8 weeks (mailed requests)

For details: CRA processing times tool

Complex adjustment requests

Complex adjustments will take longer to process due to the additional verification required.

Examples of complex requests
  • the CRA has to contact you or your authorized representative for more information or documentation
  • your request is to adjust multiple tax returns, or tax years beyond the normal 3 year reassessment period
  • your request is for a bankruptcy return
  • your request is related to a deceased taxpayer
  • you request a carryback amount such as capital or non-capital losses
  • your request is related to an international or non-resident client
  • your request is for the elected split-pension amount

Processing your request is our highest priority. The time required to complete a complex adjustment request varies with the type of adjustment and the circumstances involved.

We strive to find the right balance between processing timeliness and the need to protect the integrity of our self-assessment tax system.

After the review

The CRA will send you one of the following:

  • a notice of reassessment showing any changes to your return
  • a letter explaining why the CRA did not make the changes you asked for or if no changes were needed

To change your address, go to update your CRA information: Change your address.
