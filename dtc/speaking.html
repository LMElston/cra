Speaking eligibility

Overview

For some people, speaking can be a challenge even with the help of appropriate therapy, medication, and devices. The DTC aims to offset some of the costs caused by care, treatment, and related activities by reducing the amount of income tax you may have to pay.

Eligibility for the DTC is based on the effects of an impairment, not a diagnosis or the presence of a condition.

Example of people who may be eligible

Joseph had his larynx removed

Five years ago, Joseph had his larynx removed.

Recently, Joseph learned about the DTC and decided to apply. As a result of the information provided by his medical practitioner on the application, he is now eligible for the tax credit because he can no longer speak.

Although Joseph has just found out he is eligible, he may be able to claim the credit for the past 5 years.

Which medical practitioners can certify this
  • Medical doctor
  • Nurse practitioner
  • Speech-language pathologist

If you have 2 less severe impairments

If a single impairment does not meet the eligibility criteria for a marked restriction, you may be eligible under the cumulative effect of significant limitations, which combines 2 less severe impairments.
