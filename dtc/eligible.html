Who is eligible

Understanding eligibility

You may be eligible for the DTC if you have either a marked restriction in 1 or more of the impairment categories, or a significant limitation in 2 or more of the categories.

Select an impairment category

If you have a marked restriction in 1 category

Reveiw the categories below to find out if you have a marked restriction in 1 or more category.

What marked restriction means
  • You are unable to do the activity or it takes 3 times longer, even with the use of appropriate therapy, medication, and devices
  • This restriction exists all or almost all of the time (generally at least 90%)
  • The restriction has lasted or is expected to last for 12 consecutive months

Walking

You are unable to walk or have great trouble walking

Mental functions

You are restricted in the mental functions necessary for everyday life

Dressing

You are unable to dress yourself or have great trouble dressing

Life-sustaining therapy

You require regular therapy to support a vital function

Feeding

You are unable to feed yourself or have great trouble eating

Eliminating (bowel and bladder functions)

You are unable to manage your bowel or bladder functions or have great trouble managing them

Hearing

You have trouble understanding spoken conversation with a familiar person in a quiet setting

Speaking

When you speak, you have trouble being understood by a familiar person in a quiet setting

Vision

You are considered blind in both eyes

If you have limitations in 2 categories

Review the cumulative effect category if you have 2 or more significant limitations.

What significant limitation means
  • The 2 or more limitations exist together all or almost all of the time (generally at least 90%)
  • If the limitations were combined, their cumulative effect (combined impact) would be both of the following:
    • equivalent to being unable, or taking 3 times longer, to do the activity of 1 of the categories
    • present all or almost all of the time (generally at least 90%), even with the use of appropriate therapy, medication, and devices

Cumulative effect

You have a limitation in 2 or more categories (except life-sustaining therapy that combine to equal a marked restriction.
