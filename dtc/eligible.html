Understanding eligibility

Eligibility is based on the effects of a mental or physical impairment, and not a diagnosis or the presence of a condition.

A medical practitioner must certify that the effects of the impairment are severe, prolonged, and meet the eligibility criteria. This must be the case even with the use of therapy, medication and devices.

Applicants may apply through 1 of the 4 possible pathways to eligibility:

  • have a marked restriction in 1 or more of the DTC categories
  • have significant limitations in 2 or more of the DTC categories
  • require therapy that supports a vital function
  • be blind

Select an eligibility category

To be eligible you must be affected by at least 1 of the following categories:

Walking

You are severly restricted in walking. Examples: You are confined to a bed or wheelchair at all times or have had a limb amputated.

Mental functions

You are severly restricted in performing the mental functions necessary for everyday life. Examples: You use memory aids and adaptive aids.

Dressing

You are severly restricted in dressing. Example: You are unable to dress yourself without help.

Life sustaining therapy

You receive regular therapy at least 3 times a week and totally an average of 14 hours. Examples: Kidney dialysis, insulin therapy, oxygen therapy, or chest physiotherapy.

Feeding

You are severly restricted in feeding. Example: You require a feeding tube as your primary means of feeding yourself.

Eliminating (bowel and bladder functions)

You are severly restricted in eliminanting. Examples: You have chronic renal failure and/or require catheterization to manage my bladder functions.

Hearing

You have severe and profound hearing loss in both ears.

Speaking

You are severly restricted in speaking. Example: You use sign language as your primary means of communication.

Vision

You are considered blind in both eyes.

Cumulative (2 or more categories)

You are severly restricted in 2 or more of the categories above excluding Life Sustaining Therapy.
