Before you apply

Applying for the DTC is a 2-step process. It involves the individual who has the impairment and a medical practitioner who can certify the effects of their impairment.

Different medical practitioners can certify each impairment.

Review certifying medical practitioners
Medical practitioner Can certify
Medical doctor all impairments
Nurse practitioner all impairments
Optometrist vision
Audiologist hearing
Occupational therapist walking, feeding, dressing
Physiotherapist walking
Psychologist mental functions necessary for everyday life
Speech-language pathologist speaking

If the individual who has the impairment is not able to apply, their legal representative or a supporting family member may apply for the DTC and claim the credit on their behalf. They will have to provide some personal information, including their SIN, in the application.

Eligibility for the DTC can last several years. We recommend considering this when deciding who will claim the credit, as the CRA requires additional written confirmation to change it.

When to apply

You may apply for the DTC at any time during the year.

However, if you submit your application at the same time that you file your tax return, there may be a delay in your tax assessment. We will review your DTC application before we assess your tax return.

To avoid a possible delay, submit your DTC application before you file your tax return.

Medical practitioner fees

Some medical practitioners may charge a fee for completing the DTC application. You are responsible to pay this fee. However, you may be able to claim it as a medical expense on your tax return.

How to complete the application

There are 2 ways to complete the DTC application:

Digital application: Option 1 of 2

The medical practitioner completes Part B of the digital application first, and then gives a print out to the applicant to fill out Part A.

Apply using digital application

Paper form : Option 2 of 2

The applicant fills out Part A of the paper form first, and then gives it to the medical practitioner to complete Part B.

Apply using paper form

Regardless of the way you apply, the applicant must fill out Part A of the application, and the medical practitioner must complete and sign Part B.

Digital application

  1. Medical practitioner fills out the "Medical practitioner's section" (Part B)

    The medical practitioner opens the DTC Digital application for medical practitioners and completes Part B by answering questions about the effects of the impairment.

    Types of questions
    • Basic information about the medical practitioner
    • Patient information
    • Information about the impairment(s)
    • If your patient might be eligible under the "Cumulative effect of significant limitations" category, you will have a few additional eligibility questions to answer at the end
    How medical practitioners assess patients

    Medical practitioners use their best professional judgement when filling out Part B.

    They may base their assessment on:

    1. the symptoms reported by the patient
    2. their knowledge of the patient's medical history
    3. their knowledge of the effects of the impairment(s) as they relate to the patient's medical condition

    The CRA bases its decision about the eligibility of an applicant on the information that medical practitioners provide in Part B.

    After filling in Part B, the medical practitioner makes a PDF of the form and prints it out.

    The system does not save a copy of the responses. To keep a copy for your records, save or print the PDF.

    On the last page of the print out, the medical practitioner:

    • completes and signs the "Certification" section
    • gives the certified form to the applicant so they may fill out Part A by hand
  2. Applicant completes the "Individual's section" (Part A)

    The applicant, their legal representative, or a supporting family member fills out Part A of the printed form

  3. Send it to us

    Either the applicant or the medical practitioner may submit the form to the CRA

Paper form

  1. Applicant fills out the "Individual's section" (Part A)

    The applicant, their legal representative, or a supporting family member fills out Part A of the application:
    Form T2201, Certificate for the Disability Tax Credit

    If you want us to adjust your tax returns, indicate this in question 3 of Part A.

    After you have filled out Part A, give the printed form to your medical practitioner to complete and sign Part B.

  2. Medical practitioner completes the "Medical practitioner's" section (Part B)

    The medical practitioner answers a few questions and fills out the impairment section(s) that apply to their patient.

    Types of questions
    • Basic information about the medical practitioner
    • Patient information
    • Information about the impairment(s)
    • If the section called "Cumulative effect of significant limitations" applies, you must also fill in the individual sections for the impairments that contribute to the cumulative
    How medical practitioners assess patients

    Medical practitioners use their best professional judgement when filling out Part B.

    They may base their assessment on:

    • the symptoms reported by the patient
    • their knowledge of the patient's medical history
    • their knowledge of the effects of the impairment(s) as they relate to the patient's medical condition

    The CRA bases its decision about the eligibility of an applicant on the information that medical practitioners provide in Part B.

    The medical practitioner completes and signs the "Certification" section on the last page.

  3. Send it to us

    Either the medical practitioner or the applicant may submit the form to the CRA.

Submit the application

There are 2 ways you can submit your completed Form T2201, Disability Tax Credit Certificate and any related documents to the CRA: online and by mail.

Online:

Sign into CRA My Account and select "Submit documents" in the top right corner.

Sign in to CRA My Account Register
By mail:

Send your application by post to the nearest tax centre. We recommend you keep a copy for your records.
