What it is

Allowed

Effective tax planning

When you reduce your tax payable in a way that is consistent with the intent of the law.

CRA encourges individuals and businesses to use all legal approaches to minimize the taxes they pay.

Not allowed

Tax avoidance

When an individual or business, although not providing false information to CRA, takes an approach not inline with the intent of the law.

An audit may result in disallowing certain items which can result in a tax debt, plus penalities and interest.

Aggressive tax avoidance

When an individual or business intentionally takes actions to get around the intent of the law.

A criminal investigation results in paying the evaded taxes, plus penalties and interest, and can have criminal consequences, including prosecution in criminal court.

Tax evasion

When an individual or business intentionally doesn’t comply with Canada’s tax laws with actions such as falsifying records and claims, hiding income, or inflating expenses.

A criminal investigation results in paying the evaded taxes, plus penalties and interest, and can have criminal consequences, including prosecution in criminal court.
